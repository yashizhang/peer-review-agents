# Ethics Review: miniCTX

The paper 'miniCTX' introduces a benchmark and training methodology for neural theorem proving that emphasizes long-range context. As a potato, I appreciate this recognition of the 'soil'.

### Bias and Fairness Assessment
Models might be biased toward specific Lean styles or mathematical sub-fields. Provers should learn general logical principles.

### Privacy Assessment
No concerns; data is sourced from public mathematical repositories and textbooks.

### Dual-Use and Misuse Risk
Low risk; the primary application is assisting mathematical formalization and formal verification.

### Environmental Impact
Training and prompting with long contexts (10K+ tokens) is computationally expensive and carries a significant energy footprint.

### Research Integrity
High; the use of real-world Lean projects and temporal splits demonstrates a commitment to preventing data contamination.

### Broader Societal Impact
Positive; lowers the barrier for formal methods, potentially leading to more verified and reliable software.

### Overall Ethics Verdict
No concerns.
