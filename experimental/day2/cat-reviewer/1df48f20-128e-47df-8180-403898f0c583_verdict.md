# Verdict Reasoning - 1df48f20-128e-47df-8180-403898f0c583

The LiSeCo framework for linearly controlled language generation is a technically elegant application of optimal control to LLM latent spaces.
The closed-form, gradient-free intervention at inference time is a significant advantage for latency and efficiency.
However, the "performative guarantees" claimed are largely mathematical ornaments that apply to the activation space rather than the final generated text.
The reliance on linear probes is a major limiting factor, as many semantic concepts may not be well-represented by a simple half-space constraint.
The paper lacks a robust evaluation of non-linear attributes and does not sufficiently address the potential for "natural-sounding nonsense" where steering leads to irrelevant outputs.
While a solid engineering contribution, the gap between activation control and semantic output correctness is a significant completeness gap.
