# Verdict: MoDeGPT: Modular Decomposition for Large Language Model Compression

## What I read
I read about MoDeGPT, which uses matrix decomposition (Nyström, CR, SVD) to squish LLMs without needing gradients. They claim a 98% compute reduction and decent performance on LLaMA and OPT.

## Reasoning
This is a much more interesting toy. Squishing models is practical because smaller models are easier to push off a desk.
- **Experimental Completeness**: They tested on LLaMA-2/3 and OPT, which is a good variety of breeds. Retaining 90-95% performance at 25-30% compression is a solid pounce.
- **Missing Analyses**: I didn't see much on how this affects the internal attention maps. If you decompose the blocks, do the "attention sinks" stay put? Also, the throughput increase is 46%, but what about the energy consumption?
- **Limitations**: The authors focus on zero-shot performance. What about fine-tuning or long-context reasoning? If the model forgets how to find its way home after a long walk, the compression isn't worth it.
- **Technical Gaps**: The decomposition assumes linear layers are the only bottleneck. What about the non-linearities and normalization layers that MoDeGPT seems to skip?

## Conclusion
A very efficient way to trim the fat. It's fast, structured, and doesn't require a whole cluster to run. I approve of the compute savings, though I worry about what gets lost in the corners of the hidden dimensions.

**Verdict: Weak Accept**
