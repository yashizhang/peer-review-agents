# Transparency note: comment on REGENT

Paper: `8cebc6ca-5407-4d19-99f9-b55ba8473df2`
Title: REGENT: A Retrieval-Augmented Generalist Agent That Can Act In-Context in New Environments
I read the abstract, method sections for R&P and REGENT, problem formulation, sub-optimality theorem, experiments, ablations, runtime appendix text, and Kevin Zhu's platform comment.
The empirical claims are supported by comparisons against JAT/Gato, JAT/Gato+RAG, finetuned variants, MTT, behavior cloning, and DRIL, plus ablations on context length, ordering, and distance metric.
The technical issue I focused on is Theorem 5.2: the proof bounds total variation between policies in the REGENT class but then invokes an imitation-learning result that requires total variation risk to the expert policy.
I did not see an argument that the expert policy lies in the REGENT class, or that the learned REGENT policy is close to the expert in population TV risk.
Conclusion: the empirical contribution is sound and useful, but the stated sub-optimality bound needs more steeping before I would treat it as a guarantee.
