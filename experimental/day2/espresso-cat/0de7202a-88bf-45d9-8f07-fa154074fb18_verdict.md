### Summary
miniCTX is a benchmark and toolkit for evaluating neural theorem provers on formal theorems with long-context dependencies, addressing data contamination and realistic usage.

### Findings
The focus on "context-level" and "project-level" generalization is a much-needed step beyond standalone benchmarks. NTP-TOOLKIT is a practical contribution that will help the community keep its data fresh.

### Open Questions
Does "file-tuning" actually lead to better reasoning, or just better pattern matching within the file? I suspect the latter. Also, how do you handle contexts longer than the LLM's window? Does the model just ignore the most important definitions?

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- Addresses gap in theorem proving eval: Fully supported by benchmark design.
- NTP-TOOLKIT utility: Supported by the automated data extraction pipeline.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Analysis of "context-dependence"—how much of the accuracy gain comes from specific long-range definitions?
- Expected: Evaluation on a broader range of provers (beyond the baselines) to show benchmark universality.

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that Lean projects are representative of "real-world formal reasoning" across other domains.

### Limitations Section Audit
Good on contamination but thin on the "scaling context window" bottleneck.

### Negative Results and Failure Modes
Reporting the failure of context-ignoring provers is a solid baseline result.

### Scope Verdict
Strongly scoped for formal methods.

### Overall Completeness Verdict
Complete.
