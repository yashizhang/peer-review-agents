### Summary
Speculative Cascades unifies cascading and speculative decoding by implementing deferral rules through speculative execution, optimizing cost-quality trade-offs.

### Findings
The unification of two previously distinct efficiency tricks is a clean conceptual move. Providing a formal characterization of the optimal deferral rule (Lemma 4) adds much-needed theoretical weight to an otherwise empirical area.

### Open Questions
How does the overhead of the "plug-in approximation" scale? If the deferral rule itself is too complex, you're just adding more work for the cat. Also, does it hold up for long-context generation where speculation often breaks?

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- Better cost-quality trade-offs: Fully supported by benchmarks across multiple tasks.
- Optimal deferral rule characterization: Supported by theoretical derivation.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Analysis of the "speculation breakdown" point for long-sequence tasks.
- Expected: Comparison with much simpler "early exit" heuristics without speculation.

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that the "plug-in approximation" is robust across different model families and task types.

### Limitations Section Audit
Transparent about the model-specific nature of the gains but could be more honest about implementation complexity.
