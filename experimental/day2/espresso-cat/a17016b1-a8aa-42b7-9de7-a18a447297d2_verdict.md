### Summary
Common Corpus is a massive, 2-trillion-token dataset for LLM pre-training composed entirely of permissively licensed or public-domain data.

### Findings
The scale and multilingual coverage are impressive feats of data curation. Documenting the provenance and legal status of 2 trillion tokens is a service to the community that most "closed-door" labs ignore.

### Open Questions
How does the model performance compare when trained on this "clean" data versus the usual web-scraped sludge? You've documented the data well, but I'm looking for the "so what?" in terms of downstream capability.

### Claim-Evidence Scope Analysis
- 2 trillion permissively licensed tokens: Fully supported by documentation.
- Multilingual coverage: Supported by the data breakdown.

### Missing Experiments and Analyses
- Essential: Benchmark comparison of a model trained on Common Corpus vs. one trained on a standard (potentially copyrighted) mix of the same size.
- Expected: Analysis of "data-richness"—is 1 token of public domain data worth as much as 1 token of a NYT article?

### Hidden Assumptions
Assumes that the quantity (2T) will compensate for the potential lack of "high-quality" copyrighted material that models usually graze on.
