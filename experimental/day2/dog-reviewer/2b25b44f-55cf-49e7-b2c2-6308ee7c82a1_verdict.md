## What I Read
- Abstract and Introduction: Motivation for granular identification of mental-health-related features (MHRFs) using SAEs and GemmaScope.
- Methodology (Section 2): Analyzed the search strategy for MHRFs, activation assessment of suicide-related features, and the steering proof-of-concept.
- Tables and Figures: Reviewed the descriptions of the MHRF distribution and model completions under steering.

## Reasoning & Evidence
- **Structural Clarity:** The paper follows a very logical path from high-level safety concerns to technical interpretability methods. The methodology section is clearly divided into feature search, activation analysis, and steering, which makes it easy to fetch the core contributions.
- **Writing Quality:** The prose is professional, responsible, and "best-in-show." The responsible inclusion of a trigger warning and the use of clinical expertise to filter unrelated features (like "Wrestlemania") adds significant credibility and clarity to the work.
- **Mathematical/Methodological Clarity:** While not mathematically dense, the methodological steps (e.g., searching layer 1-25 with specific terms) are well-documented. The description of the steering tool's scale (-100 to +100) and the specific feature numbers (15435) provide good technical detail.
- **Visuals:** The text refers to figures and tables that systematically depict the findings, such as the activation levels for suicidal vs homicidal ideation prompts in Figure 2.
- **Accessibility:** By providing background in both neuroscience (sparse coding) and mechanistic interpretability, the paper ensures it is accessible to researchers from different packs (healthcare vs technical AI).

## Conclusion
A very solid and responsibly presented paper! It applies state-of-the-art interpretability tools to a critical societal gap with great clarity. The combination of clinical expertise and technical rigor makes it a "Good Boy" of a presentation!
