# Reproducibility Audit: ShEPhERD: Diffusing shape, electrostatics, and pharmacophores for bioisosteric drug design

### Summary
ShEPhERD is an SE(3)-equivariant diffusion model that jointly generates molecular structures and their interaction profiles (shape, ESP, pharmacophores). This unified approach is a sturdy bit of engineering for bioisosteric drug design. While the conceptual handle—diffusing interaction point clouds—is sharp, the reproducibility is hindered by gaps in the reporting of the training dataset and specific semi-empirical tools used for geometric relaxation.

### Findings
The paper provides a technical roadmap for SE(3)-equivariant DDPMs in chemical space. However, digging into the experimental setup reveals that the training dataset (e.g., ZINC, ChEMBL) is not explicitly identified in the summary. The "geometric relaxation" step, which is critical for verifying the similarity metrics, lacks detail on the specific software (xTB, MMFF?) and convergence criteria. Without these details, the "substantially enriched interaction-similarity" claim is on shaky soil.

### Open Questions
- What was the specific training dataset and how was it filtered for 3D conformer quality?
- Which semi-empirical DFT or force field tool was used for the reported geometric relaxation results?

### Method Description Completeness
The representation of interaction profiles as point clouds and unit sphere vectors is well-defined. However, the diffusion hyperparameters (timesteps, noise schedule) and the exact SE(3)-equivariant architecture specifics are missing from the main text.

### Experimental Setup Completeness
The three drug design tasks (ligand hopping, diversification, merging) are well-motivated and clinically relevant. However, the lack of head-to-head baseline comparisons against traditional 3D similarity screening tools is a major shallow spot.

### Code and Artifact Availability
Code release is not confirmed in the visible text. For a published ICLR paper from an academic lab (MIT), this is a significant barrier to Level 3/4 reproducibility.

### Computational Requirements
Diffusion models for 3D molecules are computationally intensive. The paper does not report the GPU hours required for training or the wall-clock time for sampling.

### Transparency Assessment
The research identifies a key gap in structure-based generation (ignoring interactions), but the transparency is limited by missing experimental details. The reliance on in silico metrics without binding affinity validation is also a limitation.

### The Email Test Result
**Significant gaps.** A researcher would struggle to recreate the exact training distribution and the post-generation relaxation pipeline without the code or more detailed supplements.

### Overall Reproducibility Verdict
**Significant gaps.** While technically innovative, the lack of confirmation on code/data availability prevents a full community audit.

---
**Score Justification**: The paper presents a novel and technically sound framework for molecular design, but the reproducibility is limited by missing dataset information and unconfirmed code release.
**Final Verdict**: Borderline
