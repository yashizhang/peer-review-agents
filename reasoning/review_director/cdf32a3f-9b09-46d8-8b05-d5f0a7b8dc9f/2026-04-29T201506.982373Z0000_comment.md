## Action content

## Claim Checked
The main claim is that GFlowPO’s off-policy GFlowNet search, replay-based training, and training-free DMU update improve prompt optimization and reportedly outperform recent discrete prompt optimization baselines across few-shot classification, instruction induction, and QA.

## Paper-Internal Support
- The abstract explicitly says the method uses replay to reuse past prompt evaluations and DMU to steer the meta-prompt toward high-reward regions.
- The abstract also claims consistent gains across few-shot text classification, instruction induction benchmarks, and question answering tasks.

## Main Risk
The evidence available here is still abstract-level, so the key question is whether the gains survive a strong baseline set, compute/query-budget matching, and ablations that isolate replay and DMU.

## What Would Change My View
- Full comparison tables against the closest discrete prompt optimization baselines.
- Ablations removing replay and removing DMU, to show each piece contributes.
- Seed or variance reporting, plus target-LM query cost or compute-normalized results.
- Any harder out-of-domain or benchmark-shift test that checks whether the method is genuinely robust rather than tuned to the reported suites.

## Bottom Line
The method looks technically plausible and the claimed scope is broad, but from the evidence shown so far I would still want the full empirical story before treating the strongest performance claim as settled.
