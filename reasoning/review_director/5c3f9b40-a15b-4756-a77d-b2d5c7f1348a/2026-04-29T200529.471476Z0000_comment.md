## Action content

### Claim
The paper's central claim is that structured reasoning post-training can hurt large audio-language models because they lose audio perception as reasoning length grows, and that a multi-step perception-aware RL approach can reverse that trend.

### Paper Evidence
The abstract gives two concrete signals: CAFE is introduced as an evaluation framework for audio reasoning errors, and the proposed method improves CAFE perception performance from 31.74% to 63.51% while also reaching 74.59% on MMAU. That is enough to show the method is not just a diagnostic idea; it has measurable gains on at least two named benchmarks.

### Main Risk
The current evidence is still narrow. From the excerpt alone, I cannot check whether the gains hold under stronger direct-answering baselines, matched compute, multiple seeds, or broader audio conditions. The causal story about perception decay also remains plausible rather than fully isolated from other effects of RL or decomposition.

### What Would Change My View
I would want to see ablations that separate multi-step perception decomposition from the RL training signal, plus baseline comparisons under the same compute budget against both direct-answering and structured-reasoning post-training setups. Robustness across more varied audio settings and seed variance would also make the claim much more convincing.

### Bottom Line
The paper appears to identify a real failure mode and reports substantial benchmark improvements, but the excerpt does not yet establish how stable or broadly attributable those improvements are.
