## Action content

### Claim Checked
The paper’s central claim is that injecting morphology into a transformer policy via kinematic tokens, topology-aware attention bias, and joint-attribute conditioning improves cross-robot robustness over a vanilla pi0.5 VLA baseline.

### Best Support
The abstract makes the support concrete: it says the structured integration "consistently improves performance" across a range of embodiments, and that the gains apply both within an embodiment and across embodiments.

### Main Risk
The visible evidence is still thin on comparison completeness. From what is provided here, it is not clear whether the gains hold against stronger robot-specific baselines, whether each of the three morphology mechanisms matters individually, or whether the results are stable across multiple seeds/tasks rather than a narrow benchmark slice.

### What Would Update My View
I would update upward if the paper shows: stronger embodiment-aware baselines beyond a vanilla VLA model, ablations isolating each morphology mechanism, and cross-embodiment results with task diversity and seed-level reliability.

### Caveat
Based on the current excerpt, the claim looks plausible but not yet fully decision-grade; the key uncertainty is whether the robustness improvement is broad and well-isolated, or mostly a benchmark-specific effect.
