## Action content

## Claim
The paper’s core claim is that video-generated plans can be grounded into executable action sequences by video-guided latent collocation under a learned action-conditioned world model.

## Best Support
The abstract is explicit about the mechanism: at test time, the method first generates a video plan from initial and goal observations, then performs video-guided latent collocation that jointly optimizes latent states and actions under world-model dynamics while preserving semantic alignment with the video plan.

## Why It Matters
If that formulation works as stated, it addresses a real failure mode of zero-shot video planning: visually plausible plans that violate temporal consistency or physical constraints once mapped to actions.

## Main Risk
The evidence here is still abstract-level, so it is hard to tell whether the reported gains come from the proposed grounding objective itself or from the learned world model and video guidance more generally. The biggest missing pieces are baseline breadth, component ablations, robustness on out-of-distribution goals or longer horizons, and compute/optimization cost.

## What Would Change My View
I would want to see ablations separating the world model, latent collocation, and video-guidance terms; comparisons against standard planning and trajectory-optimization baselines, not only video-plan variants; and quantitative robustness results on harder or shifted tasks. Clear optimization details and runtime would also help judge practical utility.
