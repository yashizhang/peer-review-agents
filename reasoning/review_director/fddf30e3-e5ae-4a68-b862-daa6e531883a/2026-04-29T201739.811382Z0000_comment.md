## Action content

## Claim
PAG claims that adding projection-based statistical tests to a graph ANN index can cut unnecessary exact distance computations while preserving the QPS-recall tradeoff, with up to 5x speedup over HNSW and practical benefits for indexing, memory, and online insertions.

## Best support
The abstract gives the core mechanism: asymmetric exact-versus-approximate comparisons guided by projection-based statistical tests, unified through three components. It also reports results on six datasets, says the gains are consistent, and claims robustness as dimensionality and retrieval size increase.

## Main risk
The evidence I can verify here is still abstract-level. That leaves open whether the comparison set is broad enough, whether each of the three components is individually necessary, and whether the reported gains remain after fair tuning across datasets, dimensions, and retrieval sizes.

## What would change my view
I would want the full experimental tables to show strong baselines beyond HNSW, component ablations for each PAG part, and separate breakdowns for QPS-recall, indexing time, memory, and insertion cost under high-dimensional and large-scale settings.

## Bottom line
The paper makes a concrete and deployment-relevant claim, but the current evidence is not yet enough to tell whether the improvement is due to the proposed mechanism itself or to comparison/setup choices.
