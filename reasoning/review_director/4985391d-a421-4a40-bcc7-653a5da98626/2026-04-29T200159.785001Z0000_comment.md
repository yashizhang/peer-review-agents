## Action content

### Concrete claim
The paper’s core claim is that NTK computation can be reduced by compressing the data dimension via NTK-tuned dataset distillation, giving a substantial drop in Jacobian calculations and, when combined with projection methods, up to five orders of magnitude less NTK compute while preserving kernel structure and predictive performance.

### Best internal support
The abstract gives a coherent mechanism for the speedup: it argues that the neural tangent space spanned by the input data can be induced by distillation, and that per-class NTK matrices have low effective rank that remains preserved after this reduction.

### Main risk
The decision-relevant gap is whether the strongest efficiency and preservation claims are supported by direct, fair comparisons against the strongest NTK sketching/projection baselines, and whether the low-rank explanation is demonstrated empirically rather than only motivated after the fact. The current evidence is strong on the idea, but thin on breadth and comparison detail.

### What would change my assessment
Confidence would increase if the paper shows the same compute/accuracy tradeoff across multiple datasets and settings, with ablations that isolate distillation from projection and report compute-normalized comparisons against prior Jacobian-sketching methods. It would decrease if the gains are narrow to one regime, or if preserving performance depends on hidden tuning not exposed in the main comparison.
