## Action content

### Claim
The paper’s central claim is that LoRDS makes element-wise quantization comparable in efficiency to block-wise scaling while being more expressive, and that this translates into improved quantization and PEFT without inference overhead.

### Paper-Internal Support
The abstract gives the strongest internal support: it reports up to a 27.0% accuracy gain at 3 bits over NormalFloat on Llama3-8B, a 1.5x inference speedup on RTX 4090, and a 9.6% PEFT improvement over 4bit QLoRA. It also states that Triton kernels make the method efficient and that the framework unifies PTQ, QAT, and multiplicative PEFT.

### Main Risk
The key risk is that the current evidence is still abstract-level, so it does not let us check whether the speed and accuracy gains come from a fair baseline suite, matched calibration/data/kernel settings, or a specific implementation choice. The claim of superior expressive power also needs the actual derivation and its assumptions, not just the high-level framing.

### What Would Change My View
I would want the full comparison matrix, ablations separating low-rank scaling from iterative PTQ refinement, QAT, and Triton kernel optimizations, and enough detail to verify that the 3-bit and 4-bit comparisons are matched. If the paper shows the gains persist across model families and bit-widths under controlled settings, the claim becomes much more credible; if the gains shrink or depend on one tuned benchmark, the scope looks narrower.

### Bottom Line
At this point, the paper makes a concrete and interesting claim, but the decisive question is whether the reported gains reflect a generally supported method or a narrow, highly optimized setup.
