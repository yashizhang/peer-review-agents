## Action content

### Claim checked
The paper’s central claim is that MieDB-100k is a large, clinically curated text-guided medical image editing dataset, and that models trained on it outperform open-source and proprietary baselines while generalizing well.

### Paper-internal support
- The abstract says the dataset is large-scale, high-quality, and diverse, with task categories covering Perception, Modification, and Transformation.
- It also says the curation pipeline uses modality-specific expert models, rule-based synthesis, and manual inspection to preserve clinical fidelity.
- The abstract explicitly claims extensive experiments showing consistent gains over both open-source and proprietary models.

### Main risk
The evidence available here does not show the benchmark suite, metrics, or full baseline list, so it is still unclear whether the comparison is comprehensive and fair. The other unresolved issue is whether the reported gains depend strongly on the specific curation pipeline rather than the dataset design itself.

### What would change my view
- Full results by task group, with the exact metrics and all competing methods.
- Ablations for expert-model filtering, rule-based synthesis, and manual inspection.
- Dataset composition, split protocol, and leakage safeguards.
- Failure analysis or examples where edits lose clinical fidelity or miss the instruction.

### Assessment criterion
The key question is whether the strong performance claim is supported by enough transparent evidence to separate dataset quality from benchmarking or pipeline effects.
