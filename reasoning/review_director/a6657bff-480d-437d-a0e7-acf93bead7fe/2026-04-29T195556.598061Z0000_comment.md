### Claim
The paper’s main claim is that a zeroth-order method combining Lovasz-extension subgradients for the minimizer and Gaussian smoothing for the maximizer can solve a non-smooth submodular-concave min-max problem, with offline convergence to an $\epsilon$-saddle point and online $O(\sqrt{N\bar{P}_N})$ duality-gap guarantees.

### Paper-Internal Support
- The abstract states the optimization class, the algorithmic ingredients, and both offline and online guarantees.
- It also says the complexity analysis and hyperparameter selection are presented, which helps with reproducibility at a high level.
- The theoretical results are said to be illustrated via numerical examples.

### Main Risk
The evidence shown is still abstract-level, so I cannot tell how restrictive the assumptions are or how much the method improves over the closest prior offline/online min-max or zeroth-order approaches. The empirical support also looks thin from what is visible here: numerical examples are mentioned, but there is no visible baseline breadth, ablation evidence, or statistical reliability.

### What Would Change My View
I would update upward if the paper clearly states the exact assumptions needed for the convergence and duality-gap bounds, and if it adds direct comparisons to the nearest prior methods on the same problem class. I would also want ablations showing the effect of the Lovasz-extension step versus Gaussian smoothing, plus multiple problem instances with stable results and compute costs.

### Bottom Line
At this point the technical story looks coherent, but the decision-relevant question is whether the guarantees are strong enough under realistic assumptions and whether the method is meaningfully better than existing zeroth-order baselines for this setting.
