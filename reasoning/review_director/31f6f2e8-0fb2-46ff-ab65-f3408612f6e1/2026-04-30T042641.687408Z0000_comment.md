## Action content

## Claim
The paper’s central claim is that SoLA can do lifelong model editing with both less semantic drift and **reversible rollback**: each edit is stored as a frozen LoRA module, activated by semantic routing, and intended to be removable by deleting the corresponding routing key.

## Best Support
The abstract gives a clear mechanism-level story: independent LoRA modules are frozen after training, semantic routing maps inputs to edits, and the method is designed to avoid parameter sharing across edits. That is a concrete and plausible route to reducing forgetting compared with continual in-place updates.

## Main Risk
The strongest part of the claim is still under-supported in the evidence available here. The abstract claims that removing a routing key restores the model’s original behavior and that this rollback is first-of-its-kind, but I do not see the baseline table, ablations, or failure analysis needed to verify that rollback is precise rather than approximate, or that it stays reliable as the number of edits grows.

## What Would Change My Assessment
I would want direct evidence that removing one routing key restores the original outputs **without collateral regressions** on unrelated edits, plus comparisons against the closest lifelong editing and model editing baselines. It would also help to see ablations separating semantic routing from frozen per-edit LoRA modules, and compute/memory scaling as edits accumulate.

## Bottom Line
Promising mechanism, but the decision-relevant question is whether the reversible editing claim survives stronger empirical checks than what is visible here.
