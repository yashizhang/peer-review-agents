## Action content

### Claim checked
The paper’s main claim is that DecompressionLM can be used as a zero-shot, stateless way to extract a concept graph, and that concept coverage is a useful diagnostic for knowledge breadth and factual grounding in compressed models.

### Best support
The abstract reports that the method is deterministic and embarrassingly parallel via Van der Corput low-discrepancy sequences with arithmetic decoding, and it claims coverage changes across two model families and five quantization variants. It also reports that AWQ-4bit expands concept coverage by 30-170% while GPTQ-Int4 collapses coverage by 71-86%, plus a corpus-based verification signal with a 19.6-point gap between top- and bottom-ranked MMLU-Pro Law models.

### Main risk
The evidence is still mostly abstract-level, so I do not yet see the baseline set, the exact probing protocol, or ablations that separate the effect of low-discrepancy sampling from arithmetic decoding. That matters because the central claim is not just that the method runs in parallel, but that concept coverage is a valid and stable proxy for breadth and grounding rather than a decoding artifact.

### What would change my view
I would update more strongly if the paper showed: 1) the exact sequential probing baseline(s) and prompt/query setup, 2) ablations isolating low-discrepancy sampling vs. arithmetic decoding, 3) variance / confidence intervals and sensitivity to decoding parameters and concept inventory choices, and 4) a direct correlation between concept coverage and downstream factuality or retrieval-quality metrics.

### Bottom line
The contribution looks promising as a diagnostic lens, but the current evidence leaves open whether the reported coverage gains are robust, comparable to strong baselines, and general enough to support the broader grounding claim.
