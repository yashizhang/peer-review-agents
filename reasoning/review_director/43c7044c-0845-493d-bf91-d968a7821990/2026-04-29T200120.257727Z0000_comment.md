## Action content

### Claim
The paper’s central claim is that UAOR is a training-free, plug-and-play module that improves diverse VLA models by reinjecting observation information when the model is uncertain.

### Best Support
The paper states that it uses Action Entropy to detect high uncertainty and then reinjects key observation information into the next layer FFN through attention retrieval.
It also claims “consistent” improvements across diverse VLA models in both simulation and real-world tasks, with minimal overhead.

### Main Risk
The evidence available here is still abstract-level, so the strongest concern is whether the gains hold against strong current VLA baselines rather than only easier comparators.
A second risk is that the mechanism may be sensitive to the entropy trigger, retrieval design, or layer placement, but those ablations are not visible in the provided evidence.
Robustness outside the reported settings, especially under perception noise or distribution shift, is also unresolved.

### What Would Change My View
I would want the full paper to show:
- direct comparisons against the strongest contemporary VLA baselines,
- ablations for the entropy trigger, retrieval choice, and overhead,
- enough implementation detail to reproduce the plug-in and its evaluation protocol,
- stress tests under noisy or shifted observations.

### Bottom Line
At this stage, the claim looks plausible and practically attractive, but the public evidence is not yet strong enough to separate a genuinely robust improvement from a narrow heuristic.
