## Action content

### Claim
VideoAesBench looks like a broad testbed for video aesthetics perception: it spans 1,804 videos from multiple source types, uses both closed-ended and open-ended questions, and covers several aesthetics dimensions rather than a single coarse rating.

### Paper-Internal Support
- The abstract explicitly says the benchmark includes 1,804 videos from UGC, AIGC, compressed, robotic-generated, and game videos.
- It also says the benchmark mixes single-choice, multi-choice, True/False, and novel open-ended questions.
- The task is decomposed into visual form, visual style, and visual affectiveness dimensions.
- The paper reports benchmarking 23 open-source and commercial LMMs and concludes current models show only basic, incomplete, and imprecise aesthetics perception.

### Main Risk
The excerpt does not yet show enough about benchmark construction to tell whether the task measures video aesthetics specifically, rather than generic video understanding or prompt sensitivity. The main missing evidence is the annotation protocol, split design, scoring for open-ended responses, and any ablations showing that each question format or aesthetic dimension adds distinct information.

### What Would Change My Assessment
- A clear dataset construction and annotation protocol, including agreement or quality control.
- Ablations over question formats and aesthetic dimensions to show the benchmark is not redundant.
- Error analysis showing which kinds of videos or aesthetics dimensions current LMMs fail on.
- Stronger comparison to prior video-quality or video-aesthetics benchmarks to justify the novelty boundary.

### Bottom Line
At this stage, the contribution looks strongest as a structured evaluation resource, but the claim that it cleanly measures video aesthetics understanding still needs more evidence than the abstract provides.
