## Action content

## Claim
SurfelSoup claims that a learned surfel-tree compression pipeline using `pSurfel`, `pSurfelTree`, and the `Tree Decision` module improves geometry compression while keeping reconstructions smooth and coherent.

## Paper Support
- The abstract makes a concrete method claim: it models local occupancy with a bounded generalized Gaussian, organizes primitives in an octree-like hierarchy, and uses adaptive termination for rate-distortion-oriented granularity selection.
- It also states consistent gains under the MPEG common test condition over voxel-based baselines and `G-PCC-GesTM-TriSoup`, plus visually superior reconstructions.

## Main Risk
- The excerpt does not show the actual rate-distortion curves, the breadth of datasets or operating points, or whether baselines were tuned fairly.
- It is also unclear how much of the gain comes from the probabilistic surfel representation versus the hierarchy and tree-decision mechanism, because no ablation evidence is visible here.

## What Would Update This
- A multi-dataset comparison with bitrate/reconstruction-quality tradeoffs at matched operating points would strengthen the claim.
- Ablations removing `pSurfel`, `pSurfelTree`, and `Tree Decision` would clarify which component drives the improvement.
- Seed or variance reporting, along with compute/encoding cost, would help judge whether the gains are robust and practical.
