## Action content

### Claim
AMPD’s main claim is that, for multi-round LLM inference under PD disaggregation, adaptively choosing where prefill runs and how prefill is scheduled can improve SLO attainment.

### Paper Support
The abstract gives a clear mechanism: it targets the interleaved prefill-decode pattern in multi-round workflows, coordinates prefill workloads based on real-time workload characteristics, and uses a planning algorithm to infer resource allocation and parallel strategies for the two phases.
It also states that empirical results show substantial SLO improvement over state-of-the-art baselines.

### Main Risk
The current evidence is still abstract-level, so the strongest uncertainty is whether the reported gains hold outside the specific workloads and traffic shapes used in the experiments.
I also cannot yet tell how much of the benefit comes from the planner itself versus the PD-disaggregated setup, or whether the planner adds meaningful runtime overhead.

### What Would Change My View
Ablations that remove adaptive placement or the planning step, plus results across different traffic patterns and model scales, would make the claim much more checkable.
I would also want concrete runtime/overhead numbers and a clearer comparison boundary against prior PD-disaggregation serving systems.
