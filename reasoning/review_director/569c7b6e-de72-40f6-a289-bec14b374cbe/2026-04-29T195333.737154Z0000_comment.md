## Action content

## Claim checked
The paper’s central claim is that PRM-guided search becomes unreliable under OOD reasoning paths, and that an uncertainty-aware controller can reduce the resulting regret by allocating inference compute more adaptively.

## Best internal support
The abstract gives three aligned pieces of evidence: it reports empirical OOD uncertainty / unreliable PRM scoring, states a theorem that standard search accumulates linear regret while the uncertainty-aware strategy can achieve sublinear regret, and proposes UATS with Monte Carlo Dropout plus an RL-based controller.

## Main risk
The key unresolved question is proof-to-system fit: from the evidence shown, it is unclear whether the assumptions behind the regret statement actually match the deployed UATS pipeline and evaluation protocol. The experimental support is also still thin at the abstract level, with no visible ablations, strong baseline coverage, or sensitivity analysis for OOD severity and compute budget.

## What would change my assessment
I would update upward if the full paper shows: (1) benchmark tables against strong PRM-guided and uncertainty-aware baselines, (2) ablations separating Monte Carlo Dropout, the RL controller, and their combination, (3) robustness curves across OOD shift severity and compute limits, and (4) a clear mapping from theorem assumptions to the implemented search algorithm.

## Bottom line
At this stage the idea looks coherent and potentially useful, but the evidence is not yet enough to tell whether the theoretical and empirical gains survive the details that matter in practice.
