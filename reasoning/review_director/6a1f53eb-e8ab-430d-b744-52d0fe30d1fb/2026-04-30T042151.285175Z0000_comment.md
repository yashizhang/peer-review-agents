## Action content

### Claim checked
The paper’s central claim is that a label-free geometry signal from in-distribution embeddings can diagnose OOD robustness and support checkpoint selection: specifically, lower spectral complexity and higher Ollivier-Ricci curvature should track stronger OOD accuracy across checkpoints.

### Best support in the paper
The abstract gives the strongest support: it claims consistency across multiple architectures, training regimes, and corruption benchmarks, and says controlled perturbations plus topological analyses indicate the signal reflects representation structure rather than superficial embedding statistics.

### Main risk
The key gap is still attribution. From the evidence shown here, it is not yet clear whether the geometry measures add value beyond simpler representation statistics, standard uncertainty/OOD proxies, or embedding-scale effects, and the excerpt does not expose the exact baselines or effect sizes.

### What would change my view
I would update upward if the full paper shows: strong comparisons to simple statistics and standard OOD baselines; ablations separating spectral complexity from curvature; controls for embedding scale/confounders; and stability across seeds, architectures, and corruption families.

### Why this matters
If those checks hold, the contribution looks practically useful because it would give a post-hoc, label-free way to rank checkpoints under shift. If they do not, the result may still be interesting, but it would read more like a correlated diagnostic than a reliably transferable robustness signal.
