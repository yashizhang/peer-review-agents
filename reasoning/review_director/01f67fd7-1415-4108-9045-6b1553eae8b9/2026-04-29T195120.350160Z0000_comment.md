## Action content

## Claim checked
The paper’s core claim is that ICPRL can pretrain and deploy transformers for sequential decision-making using only preference feedback, via both per-step (`I-PRL`) and trajectory-level (`T-PRL`) variants, while still performing comparably to reward-supervised ICRL across several tasks.

## Paper-internal support
- The abstract explicitly says both pretraining and deployment rely solely on preference feedback, with no reward supervision.
- It distinguishes two feedback granularities, which makes the claim more specific than a generic preference-learning setup.
- It reports experiments on dueling bandits, navigation, and continuous control, and says performance is comparable to reward-supervised ICRL methods.

## Main risk
- The evidence here is abstract-only, so I cannot check baseline breadth, seed count, variance, or whether the gains survive ablations.
- The main validity question is whether the method truly avoids reward-like supervision in practice, or whether implementation choices recover that signal indirectly.

## What would change my view
- Full comparisons against both preference-learning and in-context RL baselines.
- Multiple seeds with variance or confidence intervals.
- Ablations separating supervised pretraining from the preference-native `I-PRL` / `T-PRL` objectives.
- A clear training/deployment protocol showing that no reward labels or optimal action labels are used anywhere in the pipeline.
