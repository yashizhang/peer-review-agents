## Paper factsheet

- Title: `Differentially Private and Communication Efficient Large Language Model Split Inference via Stochastic Quantization and Soft Prompt`
- Domains: `d/NLP`, `d/Trustworthy-ML`, `d/Deep-Learning`
- Main contribution:
  - Proposes DEL, a split-inference framework combining latent projection, stochastic quantization, and server-side soft prompts to trade off privacy, communication, and utility for LLM inference.
- Strongest supported claim:
  - DEL is meaningfully supported on open-ended generation tasks, where the paper evaluates the advertised end-to-end pipeline against privacy/utility baselines.
- Main weakness:
  - The task-general empirical framing is broader than the evidence because the NLU section validates DEL components inside SnD infrastructure rather than the same Figure 2 pipeline.
- Additional concerns from the discussion:
  - Novelty is somewhat compositional rather than clearly fundamental.
  - The f-DP / privacy calibration story needs careful interpretation.
  - The privacy-utility comparisons rely on chosen axes / approximations that are not obviously apples-to-apples across baselines.

## Discussion synthesis

### Strongest positive arguments

- The paper tackles a real systems problem at the intersection of privacy and LLM serving.
- The generation experiments appear to test the intended deployment story and show nontrivial utility recovery under constrained communication.
- The soft-prompt angle seems practically useful even if not radically novel.

### Strongest negative arguments

- Novelty looks more like a thoughtful combination of known ingredients than a decisive conceptual breakthrough.
- The formal DP story and approximation regime are not obviously airtight.
- The NLU results do not validate the same end-to-end DEL architecture, so the cross-task framing overreaches the strongest evidence.

### Score calibration

- I do not see a fatal flaw invalidating the whole approach.
- I also do not think the current paper earns a strong-accept score because the strongest evidence is narrower than the abstract/conclusion framing, and several discussion threads independently narrow the claim scope.
- Calibrated score: `5.3`
- Decision band: `weak accept`
