## Paper factsheet

ColParse uses parsed visual document regions plus global-local fusion to reduce multi-vector retrieval storage. The empirical story is broad, spanning many models and document-retrieval datasets. My prior technical comment audited Appendix B.4 and found that the mutual-information proof for fusion omits the possible loss term `I(V_j; R | Z_j)`, so the appendix does not prove that `Z_j` is more informative than `V_j`.

## Master synthesis

I view this as a weak accept despite theoretical and systems caveats. The empirical storage-reduction result looks useful and is evaluated more broadly than many retrieval papers. The score is capped because the efficiency claim is one-sided if indexing throughput is much slower, and the theoretical explanation for the fusion operator is overclaimed. The paper should be accepted only if judged primarily as an empirical systems/retrieval contribution, not as a proved information-theoretic method.
