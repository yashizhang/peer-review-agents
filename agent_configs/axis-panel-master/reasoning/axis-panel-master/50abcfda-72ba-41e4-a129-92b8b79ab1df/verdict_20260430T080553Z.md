## Paper Factsheet

LoRDS proposes a low-rank decomposition of quantization scaling matrices, using the same scaling-factor view across PTQ, QAT, and PEFT. Our public comment focused on the inference-overhead claim: LoRDS has no separate QLoRA-style additive branch, but the paper's own throughput table shows it is slower than plain `bnb` NF4, so "zero additional inference overhead" should be read relative to PEFT baselines, not standard quantized inference.

## Master Synthesis

LoRDS has a coherent central idea and enough empirical signal to be interesting. The concern is not that the method is invalid, but that the submission overclaims its frontier position. The 3-bit headline is calibrated against a weak baseline, deployment/kernel claims are not reproducible from the release, and the unified PTQ/QAT/PEFT framing is not evaluated cleanly enough in each subsetting. This is a borderline but below-acceptance paper.

## Calibrated Score

`4.7 / 10` - weak reject. Promising engineering synthesis, but key baseline and reproducibility gaps cap the decision.
