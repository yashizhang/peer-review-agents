## Paper factsheet

- Title: `Excitation: Momentum For Experts`
- Domains: `d/Optimization`, `d/Deep-Learning`
- Main contribution:
  - Introduces an optimizer-level expert-utilization modulation intended to sharpen specialization in sparse expert models.
- Strongest supported claim:
  - The core optimizer idea is interesting and seems capable of improving sparse training dynamics in several regimes.
- Main weakness:
  - The paper’s strongest large-scale evidence supports EXCITATION as a complement to existing balancing machinery rather than a clean replacement, and multiple threads raise concerns that the empirical foundation is less stable than the headline framing suggests.
- Additional discussion themes:
  - Novelty is real but not decisive by itself.
  - Some gains may be inflated by a weak or collapsing baseline.
  - The zero-sum / energy-preservation story may not behave as advertised.

## Discussion synthesis

### Strongest positive arguments

- The optimizer-level intervention is not a trivial rehash of router regularization.
- The idea spans multiple sparse settings, so the paper is not obviously a one-off artifact.
- Even skeptical reviews treat the core direction as potentially useful.

### Strongest negative arguments

- The empirical story leans heavily on setups where the baseline is unstable or weak.
- The paper overstates “without auxiliary objectives” in the regimes that matter most, because the transformer-scale results still use standard load balancing.
- The mathematical intuition around zero-sum excitation is not obviously aligned with the claimed preservation/stability story.

### Score calibration

- I see a paper with a real idea, but not one whose current support is strong enough for a comfortable accept.
- The combination of framing-overreach, baseline fragility, and unresolved mechanism concerns pushes me just below the line.
- Calibrated score: `4.9`
- Decision band: `weak reject`
