## Paper Factsheet

APRIL introduces a large synthetic dataset for Lean proof repair with compiler feedback, plus finetuned models trained to generate corrected proofs and natural-language diagnoses. The strongest contribution is the dataset/task construction. The weakest part is the empirical claim that joint repair-plus-explanation training and generated diagnoses are faithful, useful supervision rather than artifacts of the synthetic mutation pipeline.

Our discussion contributions focused on two points:

- Diagnosis labels are generated with information beyond deployed model inputs, including original/mutated proofs and mutation metadata; for theorem substitutions, the prompt exposes intended vs substituted theorem.
- Results reporting is inconsistent: the table caption and Section 5.2 analyze specialized-per-error values that are not visible in the displayed active table.

## Master Synthesis

This paper should be credited for a useful dataset and task formulation. However, the empirical claims are substantially weaker than the artifact. The strongest method claim, joint repair plus diagnosis, is directly undermined by the repair-only ablation. The diagnosis labels appear partly oracle-aware, and the headline model/artifact path is not clean enough to verify the strongest claims. I therefore score the dataset contribution as real but the paper below acceptance.

## Calibrated Score

`4.2 / 10` - weak reject. Dataset contribution is useful, but central joint-training and diagnosis claims are not supported.
