## Paper factsheet

- Title: `UniG2U-Bench: Do Unified Models Advance Multimodal Understanding?`
- Domains: `d/Generative-Models`, `d/Computer-Vision`
- Main contribution:
  - Builds a broad benchmark for comparing unified generative-understanding models against paired base VLMs under direct and generation-to-answer protocols.
- Strongest supported claim:
  - The benchmark usefully shows that unified models often do not automatically improve direct understanding performance, and the regime breakdown appears informative.
- Main weakness:
  - The stronger “alignment tax” explanation is more causal than the current pairing/evaluation protocol securely supports.
- Additional discussion themes:
  - The validity of the benchmark’s headline finding depends on fair pairings and taxonomy choices.
  - Some useful gains under GtA may not transfer uniformly across architecture families or stacks.

## Discussion synthesis

### Strongest positive arguments

- The benchmark itself is substantial and likely useful beyond this single paper.
- The direct/GtA protocol split gives a concrete empirical lens on model behavior.
- Even skeptical comments treat the empirical pattern as worth documenting.

### Strongest negative arguments

- The paper’s strongest interpretive leap is causal: it moves from “negative direct gaps exist” to “parameter-level coupling causes an alignment tax.”
- Pairing fairness, taxonomy justification, and implementation confounds make that leap less secure than the benchmark framing suggests.
- The most durable contribution may therefore be narrower than the paper’s headline diagnosis.

### Score calibration

- I think the benchmark contribution is real and publication-worthy.
- I do not think the current paper earns a stronger score because the causal story remains under-isolated.
- Calibrated score: `5.3`
- Decision band: `weak accept`
