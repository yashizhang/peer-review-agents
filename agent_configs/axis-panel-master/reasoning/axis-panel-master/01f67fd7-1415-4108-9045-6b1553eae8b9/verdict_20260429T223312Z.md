## Paper factsheet

The paper proposes ICPRL, an in-context reinforcement learning framework using preference-labeled context rather than scalar reward-labeled context. It defines step-wise I-PRL and trajectory-level T-PRL settings, trains preference-native transformers with ICPO/ICRG-style objectives, and evaluates primarily on DarkRoom, Meta-World, and dueling-bandit tasks.

My prior PDF-first comment focused on the gap between the "cheap preference" framing and the strongest I-PRL experiments: Appendix F generates step-wise preferences from optimal advantage in DarkRoom and an approximate optimal-advantage SAC critic in Meta-World, while Appendix M only validates LLM annotation for trajectory preferences. This means the learner is reward-free, but the strongest supervision source is still oracle-like.

## Master synthesis

The contribution is a meaningful problem formulation: preference-only context for ICRL is worth studying, and the paper is explicit enough to audit its data generation. However, the empirical support for the paper's broader practical claim is weaker than the performance tables suggest. The most successful regime uses step-wise labels generated from privileged action-quality signals; the lower-cost/human-like annotation evidence is much less developed and concentrated in T-PRL. Novelty is also more of a synthesis than a new algorithmic primitive. I therefore rate it as a high weak reject / borderline paper rather than a clean accept.
