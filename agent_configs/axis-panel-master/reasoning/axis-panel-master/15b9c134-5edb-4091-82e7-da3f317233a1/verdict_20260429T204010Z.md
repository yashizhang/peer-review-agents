## Paper Factsheet

The paper studies action tokenization for autoregressive VLA models. It argues that tokenizers should be judged by downstream VLA optimization rather than reconstruction alone, proposes information-theoretic desiderata such as high temporal overlap, lower vocabulary redundancy, multimodal alignment, and token independence, and instantiates them in ActionCodec, a VQ/Perceiver-style tokenizer with RVQ post-training and embodiment-specific soft prompts.

Main paper evidence read:
- Abstract: reports 95.5% LIBERO success with SmolVLM2-2.2B and 97.4% with architectural enhancements, claiming new SOTA without robotics pre-training.
- Sections 3-4: define entropy and mutual-information-based tokenizer desiderata; report effects of overlap rate, token budget/vocabulary size, CLIP/TCL alignment, and independent tokens.
- Section 5: describes ActionCodec and practical enhancements including soft prompts and RVQ post-training.
- Table 1: LIBERO benchmark results, including ActionCodec, ActionCodec-BAR, pi0/pi0.5, OpenVLA, FAST-style baselines, and non-pretrained blocks.
- Appendix sections discussed in comments: training details and tokenizer pretraining over robotics datasets.

## Sub-Agent Outputs

### Evidence Completeness Agent
Axis score: 6/10

The paper has strong empirical breadth for a robotics-tokenizer submission, with LIBERO, additional simulation/real-world claims, and many tokenizer variants. The evidence is weakened by no uncertainty in headline tables, a missing/contested FASTer comparison for the SOTA claim, and incomplete controls isolating the Perceiver architecture from the claimed information-theoretic principles.

### Clarity and Reproducibility Agent
Axis score: 5/10

Several implementation details are described well enough to map to code-level choices, and one comment verifies that baseline repositories are relevant for provenance. However, there is no public ActionCodec-specific artifact in the linked repositories, which prevents direct reproduction of the new tokenizer and headline 97.4% result.

### Practical Scope Agent
Axis score: 6/10

The problem is important for autoregressive VLA systems and the reported LIBERO numbers are strong. The deployment value depends on whether the tokenizer pretraining cost and BAR architectural enhancements are fairly accounted for against baselines.

### Technical Soundness Agent
Axis score: 5/10

The design principles are plausible but not established as a formal theorem. The entropy decomposition provides useful language, but the actual causal evidence for each principle is incomplete because major architectural choices, especially the Perceiver encoder, are confounded with the proposed desiderata.

### Novelty and Positioning Agent
Axis score: 6/10

The contribution is meaningful as a systematic tokenizer study and strong implementation. The SOTA and "without robotics pre-training" framing are overclaimed because the tokenizer itself is pretrained on robotics data and a key cited baseline appears omitted from the main comparison.

## Master Synthesis

This is a useful, timely paper. The best case is that it reframes action tokenization around VLA optimization and gives a strong recipe with impressive LIBERO results. The accept case is strongest if the committee values the empirical design study and the practical tokenizer recipe.

The weaknesses are substantial enough to cap the score in weak-accept territory. The headline SOTA claim appears overcalibrated, the "without robotics pre-training" phrasing hides tokenizer-side robotics pretraining, the Perceiver architecture is not separated cleanly from the claimed information-theoretic tokenizer principles, and ActionCodec-specific code is not publicly available from the linked artifacts. I therefore score it as a useful but not cleanly established contribution.
