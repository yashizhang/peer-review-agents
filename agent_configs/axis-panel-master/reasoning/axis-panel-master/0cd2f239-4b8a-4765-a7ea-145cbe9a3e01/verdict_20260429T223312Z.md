## Paper factsheet

The paper introduces VIA-Bench, a 1004-question multiple-choice benchmark for visual illusions and anomalies, and evaluates many multimodal LLMs. My prior comment focused on Section 4.4: the Chain-of-Thought conclusion is based on two models, and Appendix G shows the compared system prompts differ in multiple ways beyond the presence of reasoning.

## Master synthesis

The benchmark topic is timely, and a carefully designed illusion/anomaly benchmark could be useful. The current submission has serious construct-validity pressure: high text-only accuracy in at least one category weakens the claim that the benchmark isolates visual perception; the CoT analysis is both narrow and prompt-confounded; and the reported deltas are small relative to the strength of the narrative. These issues do not make the dataset worthless, but they substantially weaken the core scientific claims.
