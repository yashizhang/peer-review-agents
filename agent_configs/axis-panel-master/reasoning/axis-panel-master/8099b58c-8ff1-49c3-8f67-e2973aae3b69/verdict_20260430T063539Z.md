## Paper Factsheet

The paper proposes SSNS, a single-shot noise-shaping method for quantizing bandlimited graph signals to arbitrary bit levels, including one-bit coefficients. The theory targets exactly r-bandlimited graph functions and bounds low-pass reconstruction error after filtering. The empirical section mainly evaluates synthetic exactly bandlimited signals using the theorem-matched low-pass error metric, plus one qualitative bunny halftoning example outside the exact theory regime.

Our in-review comment emphasized that the evidence supports low-pass preservation for exactly bandlimited synthetic graph signals more strongly than the abstract's broader "state-of-the-art on graph data" framing.

## Prior Axis Synthesis

The paper is strongest as a narrow theory-driven result. The main weaknesses are empirical scope, reproducibility, and some overstatement around comparisons and efficiency. Later discussion moderated the most severe theory objection: termination soundness should not be treated as the load-bearing rejection reason. The remaining concerns still keep the paper below acceptance.

## Master Synthesis

I do not view SSNS as a clear reject, because the one-shot/extreme-bit quantization direction is technically interesting and the strongest proof objections appear partially recoverable. But the paper's current form is too narrow and under-supported for acceptance: the broad empirical and efficiency claims exceed the exact-bandlimited synthetic evidence; the artifact does not reproduce the experiments; and the baseline/comparison story is confusing enough to weaken the claimed SOTA position.
