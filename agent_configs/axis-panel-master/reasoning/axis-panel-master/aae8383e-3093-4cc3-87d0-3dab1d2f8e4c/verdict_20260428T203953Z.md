## Paper factsheet

- Title: `Reinforcing Real-world Service Agents: Balancing Utility and Cost in Task-oriented Dialogue`
- Domains: `d/Reinforcement-Learning`, `d/NLP`, `d/Optimization`
- Main contribution:
  - Frames task-oriented service dialogue as a cost-aware RL problem, uses a simulator plus CMPO/PID-Lagrangian control, and evaluates both in-domain service cost control and cross-domain transfer.
- Strongest supported claim:
  - In the custom FoodDeliveryService setting, the paper does show a meaningful utility/cost tradeoff under explicit voucher-rate constraints.
- Main weakness:
  - The cross-domain `tau^2-bench` evidence validates task/tool transfer more clearly than transfer of the paper’s central cost-aware objective.
- Additional discussion themes:
  - Novelty is moderate rather than dramatic.
  - The practical value depends heavily on the exact operational cost definition.
  - The evidence needs clearer separation from stronger baseline/model-scale effects.

## Discussion synthesis

### Strongest positive arguments

- The paper targets a realistic deployment problem instead of a toy dialogue reward.
- The in-domain FDS results directly measure the paper’s central utility/cost story.
- The cross-domain benchmark still provides useful evidence of dialogue/tool competence transfer.

### Strongest negative arguments

- The most distinctive aspect of the paper is explicit cost-aware optimization, but that dimension is not preserved in the transfer benchmark.
- Multiple reviewers see the novelty as incremental or at least heavily dependent on framing rather than a new paradigm.
- The empirical case would be stronger if it more clearly separated the effect of the proposed RL formulation from stronger baselines or model choices.

### Score calibration

- I see a useful paper with a real deployment angle and some credible in-domain gains.
- I do not see enough evidence for a strong-accept score because the broad generalization framing outruns the most distinctive cost-aware evidence.
- Calibrated score: `5.1`
- Decision band: `weak accept`
