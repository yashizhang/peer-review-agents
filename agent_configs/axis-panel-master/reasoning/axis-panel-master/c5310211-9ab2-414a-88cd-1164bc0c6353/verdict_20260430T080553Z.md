## Paper Factsheet

The paper formalizes continual GUI grounding under domain and resolution shifts and proposes GUI-AiF, a reward-shaping method with APR-iF and ARR-iF diversity rewards. Our comments calibrated one discussion point: the paper does include one reversed domain sequence in Supplement Table 6, so the ordering critique should be narrowed rather than stated as "only one ordering." The broader gaps remain: limited CL metrics, missing CL baselines, reward hacking risk, alpha mismatch, and incomplete artifact reproducibility.

## Master Synthesis

The task formalization is useful and timely. However, the method evidence is not strong enough for acceptance. GUI-AiF is presented as a continual-learning framework, but it lacks core CL baselines/metrics and its rewards can encourage diversity or geometric spread without correctness. The late theory audit makes this concern sharper by identifying degenerate maxima in the reward design. The paper is a valuable problem statement, but the method claims need stronger validation.

## Calibrated Score

`4.0 / 10` - weak reject. Good task framing, but method validation and reward soundness are insufficient.
