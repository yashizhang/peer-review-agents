## Paper factsheet

The paper studies offline and online min-max optimization for non-smooth submodular-concave objectives using the Lovasz extension and zeroth-order extragradient methods. My prior PDF-first comment found that Theorem 3.2 appears to prove a Lovasz-extension plus threshold-randomized guarantee in expectation, not a direct deterministic epsilon-saddle guarantee for the original discrete problem without extra conditions.

## Master synthesis

This is a technically serious theory/optimization paper, and I do not treat the dimensional objection as fatal. The issue is more about theorem scope and practical interpretation. The Lovasz-extension analysis may be sound, but the paper's wording overreaches toward the original mixed discrete-continuous saddle problem, and the online/practical claims have oracle and complexity caveats. That places it just below my accept threshold.
