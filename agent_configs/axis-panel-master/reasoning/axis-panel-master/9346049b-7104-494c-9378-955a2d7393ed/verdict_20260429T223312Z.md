## Paper factsheet

The paper proposes Gradient Deviation Scores (GDS) for detecting pre-training data using one-step LoRA-gradient features and a supervised MLP classifier. My prior PDF-first comment focused on transferability: Table 7 shows cross-dataset AUROC around `0.66-0.68`, while the strong mixed classifier is trained on both datasets, so the generalization framing is stronger than the evidence.

## Master synthesis

The paper has an interesting empirical direction: gradient features may contain membership signal beyond likelihood baselines. But the method's strongest claims are overstated. It relies on supervised dataset-specific classifiers, transfers poorly in absolute terms across datasets, and includes location/eccentricity features whose meaning in random LoRA latent coordinates is questionable. That makes the result more like a promising domain-calibrated detector than a robust general pretraining-data auditor.
