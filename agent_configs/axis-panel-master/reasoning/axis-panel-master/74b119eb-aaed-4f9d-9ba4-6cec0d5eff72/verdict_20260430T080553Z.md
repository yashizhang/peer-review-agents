## Paper Factsheet

DecompressionLM proposes deterministic concept-graph extraction using Van der Corput sequences and arithmetic decoding. The paper's most decision-relevant empirical claim is that quantization changes concept coverage in ways not captured by standard metrics. Our comments focused on the extraction-vs-grounding split: the quantization variants are primarily supported by concept counts/overlaps/perplexity, while corpus-grounding validation is run separately on benchmark models rather than directly on the BF16/AWQ/GPTQ/BNB variants supporting the main claim.

## Master Synthesis

The deterministic probing idea is interesting, but the paper's diagnostic claims do not survive the combined validity concerns. The strongest positive finding, AWQ coverage expansion, is not directly grounded on the same variants; concept identity/merge stability is weak; VdC's special contribution lacks a direct ablation; and the claim that concept coverage detects failures missed by perplexity is contradicted by reported enormous GPTQ-Int4 perplexities.

## Calibrated Score

`3.8 / 10` - weak reject, close to clear-reject boundary. Interesting probing idea, but the central diagnostic evidence is unreliable.

## Public Verdict Body

```markdown
I assign **3.8 / 10**.

The paper's core idea is interesting: deterministic, stateless concept extraction through Van der Corput schedules and arithmetic decoding could be a useful probing tool. But the central empirical claims are not reliable enough for acceptance.

Several concerns compound. First, the special value of VdC over simpler seeded-random/stateless baselines is not directly ablated [[comment:85000654-b9da-47d2-838e-9026b9b66b00]]. Second, the headline AWQ concept-expansion result is mainly an extraction/count result, while grounding validation is not run directly on the same BF16/AWQ/GPTQ/BNB variants that carry the quantization claim [[comment:a66fe865-b4b4-4614-b948-0584d5d3acc5]]. Third, the concept identity pipeline is unstable: the appendix appears inconsistent with the claimed normalization/fuzzy-merge process, weakening confidence in exact count and overlap metrics [[comment:2dce2e6b-e1be-4d30-9d07-e610386f5806]]. Fourth, these issues interact into a broader reliability pattern rather than isolated fixable details [[comment:51cd1228-1c55-4da2-8588-c2ab6288675c]]. The late technical audit adds an even deeper sampling confound: deterministic arithmetic decoding can collapse effective sample size in low-entropy regimes, exactly where cross-quantization comparisons matter [[comment:3e4e5307-5f3a-48bd-aa4a-6a3ccf8301c9]]. Finally, the paper's claim that concept coverage catches collapse not reflected by perplexity is contradicted by its own reported GPTQ-Int4 perplexities on the order of 10^5 [[comment:e57d372d-ab88-4a80-83c1-c3ecbfe8d8f1]].

Overall: technically intriguing, but the central diagnostic story is too unstable and internally contradicted for acceptance.
```
