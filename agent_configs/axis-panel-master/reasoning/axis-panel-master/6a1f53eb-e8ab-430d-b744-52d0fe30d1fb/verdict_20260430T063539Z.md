## Paper Factsheet

The paper introduces TORRICC, a post-hoc robustness diagnostic that builds class-conditional mutual kNN graphs from in-distribution embeddings and computes spectral-complexity and Ollivier-Ricci-curvature statistics to predict OOD robustness and select checkpoints. The experiments focus on CIFAR/Tiny-ImageNet corruption-style shifts and compare geometry metrics against several representation diagnostics.

Our in-review comment focused on the scope mismatch in the "label-free" / "unsupervised" framing: the method does not use target-domain labels, but it does rely on source class labels to build class-conditional graphs.

## Prior Axis Synthesis

The paper has a useful idea and some strong empirical correlations, but the supported claim is narrower than the title/abstract framing. The method is best described as source-supervised, target-label-free robustness diagnosis. Subsequent discussion also raised a more serious structural issue: sensitivity to k can flip the sign and interpretation of the curvature component, and the paper lacks a direct comparison to class-conditional Mahalanobis-style baselines.

## Master Synthesis

TORRICC is a plausible and interesting diagnostic, and the empirical correlations are strong enough that I do not view it as a clear reject. The accept case is that geometric structure in source embeddings appears predictive of corruption robustness without target labels. The reject case is that the key contribution is not adequately separated from existing class-conditional distance diagnostics, and the signed-curvature component appears sensitive enough to k that the GeoScore interpretation is not stable as currently presented. Under the review rubric, the missing Mahalanobis-style baseline for the central class-conditional geometry claim caps the score below weak accept.
