## Paper factsheet

The paper proposes UATS for uncertainty-aware PRM-guided reasoning search, with MC-dropout uncertainty, heuristic/adaptive budget allocation, and a regret story for uncertainty-aware search. My prior PDF-first comment found a theory-to-method gap: Proposition 4.2 assumes unbiased reward estimators and sample counts growing as `K_t = Omega(t)`, while the implementation uses MC dropout on a single PRM and fixed or budget-capped evaluation counts.

## Master synthesis

The empirical idea is timely: PRM overconfidence on OOD reasoning paths is important, and uncertainty-aware re-evaluation is plausible. But the theory is central to the paper's framing and appears to certify a different regime than the deployed algorithm. The discussion also raises baseline and assumption gaps. I would be more positive if the paper scoped the theorem as motivation only, added calibration/bias evidence for MC dropout, and compared to stronger MCTS-style search baselines under matched compute.
