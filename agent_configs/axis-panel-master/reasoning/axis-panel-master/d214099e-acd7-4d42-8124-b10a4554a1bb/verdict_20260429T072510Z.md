## Paper factsheet

The paper studies MoE diffusion LLM inference, where block-parallel decoding activates many unique experts and can become memory-bandwidth bound. It proposes Dynamic Expert Sharing (DES), a sequence-level expert coreset method with two variants: DES-Seq and DES-Vote.

Main claimed contribution:
- Identify "expert explosion" in parallel MoE dLLM decoding.
- Shift optimization from token-local expert skipping to sequence-level coreset selection.
- Show DES reduces unique expert activations and MoE-layer latency while retaining accuracy.

Evidence read:
- Abstract and Section 1 describe the motivation and headline result: over 55% fewer unique experts and up to 38% latency reduction while retaining 99% relative accuracy.
- Section 2 positions DES against dynamic expert skipping and OEA.
- Section 4 defines sequence-level coreset selection and the two DES variants.
- Section 5 reports experiments on LLaDA2.0-Mini and LLaDA-MoE with HumanEval, MBPP, GSM8K, and MATH500. Table 1 reports relative accuracy and average activated experts. Figure 5 separates MoE kernel latency from end-to-end GPU kernel time.

Most decision-relevant observations:
- The idea is technically sensible and well positioned for parallel MoE decoding.
- Table 1 reports relative accuracy only, including values above 100%, without raw scores or variance.
- The strongest headline latency number is MoE-kernel latency; end-to-end GPU kernel speedup is materially smaller at 8.2%-14.3%.
- The stated multi-GPU/CPU-offload motivation is not directly evaluated.

## Master synthesis

DES is a useful systems idea: expert sharing at the parallel-block level directly targets the memory traffic bottleneck that token-local skipping misses. I am above reject because the mechanism is coherent, the prior-work positioning is careful, and the reported relative-quality retention is promising. I am not higher because the evidence package is narrow for a systems claim: relative-only accuracy, no variance, small benchmark suite, and a gap between the 38% MoE-kernel headline and the smaller end-to-end speedup. This is a weak accept if claims are qualified; it would be stronger with raw scores, seeds, sensitivity, and the multi-GPU/offload evaluations implied by the motivation.
