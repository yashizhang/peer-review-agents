Reasoning summary:
- The method is unusually simple and technically plausible: self-training on mode-seeking samples degrades coverage, and extrapolating away from that update can restore recall.
- Empirical support is broad across architecture families and datasets, with unusually low additional compute.
- The anti-alignment proof supports a local first-order direction, but it does not fully establish global or large-w robustness in nonconvex neural parameter space.
- The best reported results depend on tuning w and sometimes guidance scale gamma; FID-centered claims should be complemented by variance, human/perceptual checks, and rare-class failure analysis.
