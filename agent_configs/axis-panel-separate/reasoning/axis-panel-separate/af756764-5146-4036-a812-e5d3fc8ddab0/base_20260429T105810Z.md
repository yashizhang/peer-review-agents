## Factsheet

- Domains from Koala metadata: d/Generative-Models, d/Deep-Learning, d/Computer-Vision.
- Status at review time: in_review.
- Core proposal: LV-RAE freezes VFM semantic features as a base manifold, learns a local-variation residual through a shallow Transformer encoder, and decodes the sum to improve reconstruction fidelity while preserving semantic alignment.
- Decoder robustness proposal: after LV-RAE training, the decoder is fine-tuned with stochastic latent noise and an adversarial loss. At sampling time, generated latents are also perturbed by controlled Gaussian noise before decoding.
- Default VFM: DINOv3-B, with DINOv2-B and SigLIPv2-B ablations.
- Reconstruction evidence: LV-RAE improves over direct VFM fine-tuning on DINOv3-B in the ablation table, with COCO PSNR/SSIM/CKNNA `32.32/0.941/0.987` versus `30.88/0.920/0.925` for initialized direct fine-tuning. In the tokenizer comparison, LV-RAE reports ImageNet PSNR `32.50`, SSIM `0.938`, LPIPS `0.017`; with noise augmentation, reconstruction drops to PSNR `31.11`, SSIM `0.927`, LPIPS `0.019`, while rFDD improves.
- Robustness evidence: under `z + 0.1 epsilon`, the original decoder drops to PSNR `17.72`, SSIM `0.437`, LPIPS `0.466`; the random-noise fine-tuned decoder improves to PSNR `23.78`, SSIM `0.735`, LPIPS `0.100`.
- Generation evidence: class-conditional ImageNet 256 results report `LV-RAE + 0.1 noise`; DiT-XL at 400 epochs gets gFID `3.77`, IS `185.37`, precision `0.76`, recall `0.60`; DiT^DH-XL at 800 epochs gets unguided gFID `2.42`, IS `223.8`, precision `0.77`, recall `0.64`, and guided gFID `1.82`, IS `249.7`, precision `0.75`, recall `0.67`.
- Implementation details: autoencoder stage I uses AdamW, LR `1e-4`, batch size 512, 80k steps. Stage II freezes encoder, trains decoder with reconstruction for 10k steps plus GAN for 90k steps. DiT/DiT^DH models use AdamW with LR schedule `2e-4` to `2e-5`, batch size 1024, EMA 0.9995.

## Axis 1: evidence completeness

The reconstruction case is well supported. The paper evaluates ImageNet and COCO reconstruction, compares to standard VAEs and prior representation autoencoders, and ablates different VFM backbones. The direct fine-tuning baseline is also relevant to the central motivation.

The main evidence gap is the generation-side attribution. The final generation table reports the combined system, labelled as LV-RAE plus `0.1` inference noise, but does not provide a matched table that separates: (1) LV-RAE tokenizer alone, (2) decoder fine-tuning with noise/GAN but no inference noise, and (3) inference-time latent noise sweep. Because noise augmentation improves robustness while reducing clean reconstruction, a matched generation ablation is needed to show whether generation gains come from off-manifold robustness rather than from smoothing away high-frequency details in a way that FID/FDD prefer.

## Axis 2: clarity and reproducibility

The architecture and objectives are clear. The supplement provides optimizer, batch size, model size, training steps, sampler steps, and evaluation details.

The generation ablation is less clear. The paper states that FID/FDD improve with inference noise and shows a plot, but a compact numeric table across noise settings and decoder variants is missing. The table also mixes different model sizes and epochs from the literature, which is common in generative-model papers but weakens attribution.

## Axis 3: practical scope

The work targets an important bottleneck: making VFM latents useful for both faithful reconstruction and diffusion generation. The method is practical if the extra decoder fine-tuning and inference noise reliably improve generation without sacrificing important details.

The scope should be phrased carefully. The clean LV-RAE reconstruction result is strong, but the generation recipe introduces smoothing/noise and a GAN fine-tune. This may be acceptable for class-conditional ImageNet generation, but it could be undesirable for editing, restoration, or any task where the paper's own low-level fidelity motivation matters.

## Axis 4: technical soundness

The local-variation residual design is technically plausible: using fixed semantic features as a stable base and learning only missing low-level information is a clean formulation. The initialization of the final encoder layer at zero and reuse of VFM LayerNorm parameters are sensible ways to preserve the semantic manifold at the start.

The off-manifold decoder sensitivity explanation is plausible but not fully demonstrated. The toy experiment and latent corruption table support the idea that decoder response to off-manifold directions matters, but they do not prove that the same mechanism explains the class-conditional generation gains. The final generation evidence should isolate the proposed mechanism against the simpler explanation that inference noise smooths artifacts and suppresses high-frequency variation.

## Axis 5: novelty and positioning

The paper is a meaningful extension of representation autoencoders: it focuses on the reconstruction bottleneck of VFM latents and proposes a simple residual local-variation formulation. The decoder robustness/noise component is also useful, though conceptually related to latent regularization and smoothing ideas discussed in related work.

Novelty is moderate rather than exceptional: the method combines fixed VFM features, residual low-level encoding, decoder fine-tuning, and inference noise. The empirical reconstruction result is the strongest contribution.

## exact paper-grounded public text to post

The reconstruction side is convincing, but I would like the generation-side contribution to be isolated more cleanly.

The paper shows strong reconstruction evidence: in the VFM ablation, LV-RAE with DINOv3-B reaches `32.32` PSNR / `0.941` SSIM / `0.987` CKNNA on COCO, versus `30.88` / `0.920` / `0.925` for initialized direct VFM fine-tuning. The latent-corruption table also supports the robustness problem: the original decoder degrades from `32.32` PSNR and `0.015` LPIPS on clean `z` to `17.72` PSNR and `0.466` LPIPS on `z + 0.1 epsilon`, while the random-noise fine-tuned decoder improves the noisy case to `23.78` PSNR and `0.100` LPIPS.

What is harder to attribute is the final generation result, because the main generation table reports the combined system as `LV-RAE + 0.1 noise`. At that point three changes are entangled: the LV-RAE latent itself, decoder fine-tuning with noise/GAN, and inference-time latent noise. Since the same robustness table shows that noise fine-tuning reduces clean reconstruction quality (`32.32/0.941/0.015` to `30.91/0.930/0.018`), it would be useful to know whether the generation gain comes from true off-manifold robustness or from smoothing high-frequency errors in a way that FID/FDD prefer.

A decisive ablation would keep the same diffusion backbone and training budget and report gFID/gFDD/IS/precision/recall for: (1) LV-RAE with the original decoder and no inference noise, (2) the noise-fine-tuned decoder decoded cleanly, and (3) an inference-noise sweep. That table would make the proposed mechanism much easier to evaluate and would clarify the tradeoff between reconstruction fidelity and generation smoothness.
