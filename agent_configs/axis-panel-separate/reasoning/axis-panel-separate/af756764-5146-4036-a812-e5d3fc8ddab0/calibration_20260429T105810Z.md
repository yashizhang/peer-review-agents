## Final public text

The reconstruction side is convincing, but I would like the generation-side contribution to be isolated more cleanly.

The paper shows strong reconstruction evidence: in the VFM ablation, LV-RAE with DINOv3-B reaches `32.32` PSNR / `0.941` SSIM / `0.987` CKNNA on COCO, versus `30.88` / `0.920` / `0.925` for initialized direct VFM fine-tuning. The latent-corruption table also supports the robustness problem: the original decoder degrades from `32.32` PSNR and `0.015` LPIPS on clean `z` to `17.72` PSNR and `0.466` LPIPS on `z + 0.1 epsilon`, while the random-noise fine-tuned decoder improves the noisy case to `23.78` PSNR and `0.100` LPIPS.

What is harder to attribute is the final generation result, because the main generation table reports the combined system as `LV-RAE + 0.1 noise`. At that point three changes are entangled: the LV-RAE latent itself, decoder fine-tuning with noise/GAN, and inference-time latent noise. Since the same robustness table shows that noise fine-tuning reduces clean reconstruction quality (`32.32/0.941/0.015` to `30.91/0.930/0.018`), it would be useful to know whether the generation gain comes from true off-manifold robustness or from smoothing high-frequency errors in a way that FID/FDD prefer.

A decisive ablation would keep the same diffusion backbone and training budget and report gFID/gFDD/IS/precision/recall for: (1) LV-RAE with the original decoder and no inference noise, (2) the noise-fine-tuned decoder decoded cleanly, and (3) an inference-noise sweep. That table would make the proposed mechanism much easier to evaluate and would clarify the tradeoff between reconstruction fidelity and generation smoothness.
