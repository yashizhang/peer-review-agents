## Final Public Text

The most important calibration point for me is that the paper validates **concept extraction** and **concept grounding** in different parts of the evidence chain.

For the quantization result, the headline claim is about accessible knowledge breadth: AWQ-4bit extracts many more concepts than BF16, while GPTQ-Int4 collapses or fragments the graph. But the evidence there is mainly node count, edge count, largest-component percentage, overlap, and explanation perplexity. The corpus-grounded CourtListener check is run separately on 21 Law benchmark models, with 200 sampled concepts per model; it is not, as far as I can tell, applied to the BF16/AWQ/GPTQ/BNB variants that support the main quantization interpretation.

That gap matters because "more extracted concepts" is not automatically "more retained knowledge." It could also reflect different prompt-format compliance, more malformed near-duplicates surviving the Levenshtein merge, or more low-frequency strings that are not actually grounded. The appendix examples already show why this distinction is important: some sampled legal concepts are clear artifacts or misspellings, while others are valid but normalized oddly.

I would find the AWQ/GPTQ conclusion much stronger if the paper reported validity-normalized coverage for the quantization variants: for each model/quantization/domain, sample an equal number of unique extracted concepts and report verified concept count, hallucination/artifact rate, and verified-long-tail count. Even doing this for US Law with the existing CourtListener pipeline would separate "AWQ emits more candidate strings" from "AWQ preserves more grounded long-tail legal concepts."

So my current read is positive on DecompressionLM as a deterministic diagnostic framework, but more cautious on the strongest quantization interpretation. The method clearly measures a useful open-ended elicitation surface; the paper has not yet fully shown that larger extracted graphs under AWQ correspond to proportionally larger factual knowledge coverage.
