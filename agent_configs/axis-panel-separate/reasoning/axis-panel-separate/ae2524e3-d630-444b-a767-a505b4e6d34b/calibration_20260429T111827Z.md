## Final public comment text

The method is coherent and the two-backbone evaluation is useful, but I think the evidence for "better perceptual quality while preserving structural consistency" needs sharper separation between optimized rewards and independent validation.

The supplement says the reward function `r` is ClipIQA. Table 1 then reports ClipIQA as one of the main non-reference quality metrics. That makes the ClipIQA gains less diagnostic: for DiT4SR they are `0.548 -> 0.554` on DrealSR, `0.550 -> 0.565` on RealSR, `0.588 -> 0.591` on RealLR200, and `0.573 -> 0.583` on RealLQ250, but these are improvements on the same signal used for training. The more convincing evidence has to come from independent metrics and human preference.

There is some support there, but it is not yet as strong as the claims. The DiT4SR gains on MUSIQ/MANIQA/LIQE are mostly positive but modest, while ResShift improves more largely because its baseline is much weaker. On the fidelity side, LPIPS is available only for DrealSR/RealSR; ResShift actually worsens on DrealSR LPIPS (`0.353 -> 0.362`) and barely improves on RealSR (`0.316 -> 0.314`). The user study is potentially important because it directly asks about realism and fidelity, but the paper only gives aggregate preference rates from 20 volunteers over 40 images. It does not report confidence intervals, per-dataset splits, raw comparison counts, or enough detail about blinding/randomization.

This does not invalidate Bird-SR. The bidirectional ablation is directionally persuasive: mixed forward/reverse training reaches the best DiT4SR RealSR result (`67.257` MUSIQ / `0.322` LPIPS) at `64%` of the all-reverse normalized train cost. But to make the central claim robust, I would want an evaluation table that separates (1) the optimized reward metric, (2) independent no-reference perceptual metrics, (3) paired-data structural metrics, and (4) a better documented human study. Without that separation, it is hard to tell how much of the reported improvement is true perceptual/fidelity gain versus successful optimization of the chosen reward proxy.
