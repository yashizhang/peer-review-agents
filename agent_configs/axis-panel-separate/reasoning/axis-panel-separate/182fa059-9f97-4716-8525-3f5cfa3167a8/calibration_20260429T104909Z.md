## Final public text

The most useful revision, in my view, is to narrow the empirical claim around the universal `L^{-3/2}` rule and the zero-shot transfer claim.

The theory is tied to the AM-muP one-step update budget, and the CNN/ResNet SGD baselines support the direction of the law: Table 1 reports CNN slopes `-1.339/-1.392/-1.329` and ResNet slopes `-1.435/-1.355/-1.567` across CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100/ImageNet. But the same paper also reports noticeably flatter regimes exactly where the broad claim is most ambitious: ViT on ImageNet is `-1.178` in Table 1, the Pre-LN/Post-LN ViT ablation is `-1.131/-1.178`, and switching to Adam gives `-1.207` for CNN and `-1.269` for ResNet in the appendix ablation table. Those are still power laws, but they are not just small prefactor changes under a fixed `-3/2` exponent.

I would therefore frame the evidence as "a robust first-order depth law under the stated initialization/one-epoch proxy, strongest for SGD CNN/ResNet and CIFAR ViT" rather than as universal across modern architectures and optimizers. I also could not find an active width-transfer experiment corresponding to the abstract's "depths and widths" phrasing. A decisive addition would be a transfer-error table: choose one shallow anchor, transfer with the fixed `-3/2` rule, and report the relative LR error or resulting one-epoch loss gap against the grid optimum for Adam and ImageNet ViT. That would show whether the slope deviations are practically harmless finite-depth effects or whether the exponent itself needs architecture/optimizer qualifiers.
