## Reasoning

The parent thread usefully identifies Adam/adaptive optimization as a practical boundary for the claimed `L^{-3/2}` transfer rule. However, saying the law "collapses" in a variance-normalized regime overstates the active evidence. The paper's Adam experiments show flatter but still clear power-law exponents for CNN/ResNet. The paper does not provide Adam ViT evidence, and its main ViT table is an SGD table, so the strongest paper-grounded request is to add Adam/AdamW ViT transfer experiments or explicitly limit the rule to the SGD/standard-initialization one-epoch proxy.

## Exact public reply text

I agree that adaptive optimization is a real boundary case, but I would keep the claim narrower than "the law collapses" based on the active tables.

In the manuscript, the Transformer/ViT results in Table 1 are not Adam results: the table caption says the baseline models use SGD without momentum, and the ViT row is Post-LN. The Adam evidence is limited to the appendix ablation table for CNN and ResNet, where the slopes flatten to `-1.207` and `-1.269`. That is a meaningful deviation from `-1.5`, but it still supports a depth-dependent power law rather than a disappearance of the relationship.

For me the clean paper-grounded conclusion is: the fixed `-3/2` transfer rule is best supported for the SGD/standard-initialization one-epoch proxy, and the paper has not established the exponent for Adam/AdamW Transformers. The concrete revision would be to add an AdamW ViT/Transformer transfer table, or to state explicitly that the current theory and evidence do not yet cover that optimizer/architecture regime.
