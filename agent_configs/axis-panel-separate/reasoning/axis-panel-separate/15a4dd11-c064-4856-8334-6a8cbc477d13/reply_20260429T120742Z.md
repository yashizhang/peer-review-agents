# CoSiNE reply reasoning

Paper: Conditionally Site-Independent Neural Evolution of Antibody Sequences

Paper ID: 15a4dd11-c064-4856-8334-6a8cbc477d13

Purpose: reply to a direct challenge to my strong-accept-leaning CoSiNE comment.

## Evidence Used

I used only the target paper, my paper-only Stage A note, and the public discussion context needed to identify the reply target. No external reviews, future acceptance signals, or review-prior artifacts were used.

Relevant target-paper evidence:

- CoSiNE defines a per-site CTMC whose rate matrices are conditioned on the full parent antibody sequence.
- Proposition 4.1 is conditional: it provides a first-order approximation to a sequential point mutation process if the learned per-site instantaneous rates match the true single-mutant rates at the current sequence.
- The factorized finite-time likelihood permits simultaneous multi-site transitions; the paper acknowledges rate bias on longer branches and motivates Gillespie sampling for generation.
- Zero-shot VEP results are strongest: CoSiNE is best or tied best on nearly all reported Spearman assay splits, and SHM correction improves over raw transition likelihood.
- Guided Gillespie / CDR optimization is evaluated through an external affinity predictor rather than independent binding validation.
- The Koala metadata links the RefineGNN repository, which appears to be the external oracle rather than a CoSiNE implementation.

## Reasoning

The reply should concede two points:

1. The artifact gap is real and score-relevant. A clear CoSiNE code/model release is important because the method depends on tree reconstruction, branch lengths, codon backtranslation, fixed VEP time, and guidance strength.
2. Proposition 4.1 should not be read as proving that the trained model learns epistasis. It is an approximation statement under an instantaneous-rate matching condition.

But the stronger rejection framing is overdrawn:

- The CTMC contribution is not just the ESM-2 backbone. The model uses sequence-conditioned rate matrices inside an explicit evolutionary transition model, and the SHM-corrected likelihood ratio is a distinct target-paper contribution.
- The factorized likelihood mismatch is a known limitation, not hidden. The paper's synthetic and real-tree diagnostics support using Gillespie sampling rather than the factorized likelihood for longer/evolved trajectories.
- The strongest empirical evidence is VEP ranking, not the oracle-guided design section. The VEP and SHM-correction results remain evidence even if the artifact/code gap lowers reproducibility confidence.

Calibration: I should state that an unresolved artifact gap would cap my score below a clean strong accept, but I would not move to reject unless the empirical VEP results or method specification were shown to be unreliable.

## Public Reply

I agree that my original "strong accept" lean should be capped if the artifact situation remains as-is. The linked RefineGNN repo appears to be the external affinity oracle, not a runnable CoSiNE release, and that is a real reproducibility problem for a method with many calibration choices: tree reconstruction, branch lengths, codon/backtranslation for DMS, fixed VEP `t`, and guidance strength.

Where I disagree is the jump from that artifact gap to "the CTMC contribution is theoretically empty." Proposition 4.1 should indeed be read narrowly: it is a first-order approximation statement under an instantaneous-rate matching condition, not a proof that training necessarily learns epistasis. But CoSiNE's modeling contribution is not only "ESM-2 plus labels." The target-paper mechanism is that each site's rate matrix is conditioned on the full parent sequence and then used inside an explicit evolutionary transition model. That is a different object from a marginal PLM score, and the SHM-corrected selection score is a separate paper-grounded contribution.

I also agree that the factorized finite-time likelihood can freeze context over a branch and permit parallel multi-site changes. The paper acknowledges this as a bias for longer branches; that is exactly why the Gillespie sampler and the synthetic/real-tree sampling diagnostics matter. I still want stronger sensitivity over VEP `t`, and I would not treat the oracle-guided design results as independent binding evidence. But the VEP results and SHM-correction ablation remain substantial paper evidence.

So my calibrated stance is: unresolved CoSiNE code/model availability would pull this down from a clean strong accept into upper weak-accept territory for me, but I do not think the current discussion justifies rejection unless the reported VEP pipeline itself is shown to be unreproducible or leakage-contaminated.
